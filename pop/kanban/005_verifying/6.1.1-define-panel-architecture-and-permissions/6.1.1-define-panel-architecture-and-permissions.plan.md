# Plan — [[6.1.1-define-panel-architecture-and-permissions]]

- **Stage:** 002_planning · **Owner:** planning agent

## Objective and expected result

- **Objective:** establish the durable administrator/merchant capability matrix, canonical panel route map, denial semantics, and compatibility boundary that later panel tasks must implement.
- **Observable result:** the administrative contracts unambiguously assign every current and planned authenticated surface to `ADMIN`, `USER` (merchant), or shared authentication infrastructure, while preserving public/sessionless routes and existing opaque security outcomes.

## Strategy

Treat the closed `ADMIN`/`USER` role set as two mutually exclusive authenticated personas, not as an inheritance hierarchy. Keep `/` as the compatibility entry point: it must resolve the principal before merchant work, send administrators to `/admin`, and admit only active `USER` principals to merchant content. Canonical merchant domains remain unprefixed; administrative domains remain under `/admin`. Public checkout/store routes and protocol APIs remain outside both panels.

Specify the complete route-family matrix before changing runtime authorization. For each family, record canonical owner, allowed operations, unauthenticated outcome, wrong-role outcome, and whether compatibility requires redirect, stable endpoint, opaque unavailable state, or permanent absence. Preserve existing public identifiers, owner scoping, projections, POST response shapes, unprefixed i18n, and `[lang]` 404 stubs; do not invent authorization behavior for later feature details.

## Affected areas

- `pop/specs/administrative-foundation.md` — durable role, capability, landing, route-ownership, and denial contract.
- `pop/specs/administrative-design-system.md` — shared visual-source versus role-specific shell/navigation boundary.
- `AGENTS.md` — concise DOX summary of the approved role-exclusive route contract and pointers to any affected subtree contract.
- Current `src/app/` route inventory — read-only evidence used to prove that the compatibility matrix has no omitted authenticated route family.

## Risks and abort conditions

- **Risk:** a route family is labeled “shared” and accidentally preserves administrator access to merchant data — mitigation: shared means authentication/session infrastructure only; every business read or mutation has exactly one panel owner.
- **Risk:** a compatibility redirect performs merchant service work before resolving the role — mitigation: require role resolution before any owner-scoped read and preserve opaque protected outcomes.
- **Abort if:** a current authenticated route cannot be assigned without changing a public identifier, existing order projection, or protocol response contract — report `BLOCKED` with the route and conflicting contract instead of weakening either invariant.
