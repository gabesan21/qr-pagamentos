# 4.1.1-owner-isolation-and-checkout-policy — Plan

## Objective and expected result

Make every `Product` and `PaymentLink` belong to one persisted active-account identity, and make every product/link operation resolve through the authenticated owner. A signed-in account manages only its own catalog and links; an administrator retains the separate global administration/catalog role but does not receive an implicit cross-owner mutation path in this slice. Existing public link resolution remains sessionless, redacted, and unchanged.

Persist one closed checkout-data policy per account, defaulting to `NONE`: `NONE`, `NAME_EMAIL`, `EMAIL`, `NAME_EMAIL_CPF`, or `NAME_EMAIL_CPF_ADDRESS`. The authenticated account can inspect and change only its own policy through the existing unprefixed account shell. No checkout page, order, customer-data submission, provider call, or public policy disclosure is introduced.

## Safe strategy and durable contracts

1. Add required `owner_id` foreign keys to products and links in one immutable forward migration; the Epoch 4 decision that these tables have no production rows permits no legacy owner backfill. The migration must fail rather than invent an owner if that premise is false.
2. Make the payment link's owner and referenced product owner identical in the database (a composite product/owner reference, supported by a unique product/owner key). Service predicates also bind every list, dependency choice, update, revocation, and delete to `actor.id`; browser IDs are never an authorization source.
3. Store the closed policy on `User` with database default `NONE` and a named check. A small authenticated service validates the closed value and exposes only the actor's current policy; the public resolver neither selects nor returns it.
4. The linked specs now state the same-owner invariant and role boundary: account owners manage only their products/links, while `/admin` retains user, global payment-setting, and Nautt catalog authority without an implicit cross-owner product/link path. The two affected draft specs remain `rascunho` until the renewed yolo 003 approval promotes them; the already approved catalog spec records the corrected boundary.
5. Move product/link management from the administrator-only shell to the authenticated owner shell. Keep administrator account, global payment settings, and Nautt catalog operations in `/admin`; use new unlocalized protected mutation routes for owner resources and opaque relative redirects. Re-authorize before parsing form data or performing a target lookup.
6. Keep the existing public resolution DTO and its no-store, no-write contract intact. Checkout/order/provider code belongs to tasks 4.1.2 and 4.2.x.

## Risks, limits, and abort conditions

- **Owner mismatch or bypass:** a link that references another account's product breaks the trusted checkout-owner premise. The composite database relation and owner-constrained service queries are both required; abort to 002 if Prisma/migration design cannot express and test both.
- **Unexpected legacy rows:** no migration may silently assign all existing records to an administrator. Abort before data mutation if the no-production-rows premise is false.
- **Privilege/disclosure regression:** a user must not learn whether another account's record exists. Protected routes re-authorize first and map malformed, foreign, missing, stale, and inactive targets to the same opaque owner outcome; no `ownerId` comes from a form/query/path for authorization.
- **Scope creep:** do not change `GET /api/payment-links/[identifier]`, add policy to public data, derive payment inputs from the browser, create orders, consume a single-use link, or call Nautt.
- **UI drift:** reuse the PIX-ledger primitives/tokens and one-primary-action rule; no new primitive or raw style value. The account surface must retain localized default, pending/disabled, empty, success, error/recovery, and visible focus states.
- **Authorized verification limit:** Docker, `pnpm db:test`, and browser/screenshot evidence are explicitly skipped by the user for this task. They are recorded as deferred evidence, not substituted by a claim of runtime coverage. Static, unit, type, lint, test, and production-build gates remain mandatory.
