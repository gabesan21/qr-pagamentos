## Round 1 — 2026-07-17

### Plan summary

Implement an unlocalized central webhook route that authenticates unchanged raw bytes against all active encrypted owner secrets before parsing and accepts exactly one match. Persist a race-safe delivery/attempt ledger, then reconcile only through one owner-bound provider GET and the existing versioned CAS; duplicates, unknown/final orders, and forged requests perform zero provider reads. Database ACL/concurrency, strict outcome, 15-second/cost, aggregate, DOX, and spec-closeout gates are executable in [[2.3.1-verify-and-handle-nautt-webhooks.plan]].

### Resposta do crítico (yolo)

Decision: returned by the critic (round 1).

- **Blocking — the actual-byte ceiling is not executable as planned:** the route mandates `request.arrayBuffer()` before checking the 256 KiB actual size (`2.3.1-verify-and-handle-nautt-webhooks.plan.md:30`), so a missing or dishonest `Content-Length` makes the runtime buffer the complete unbounded body before it can reject it. This contradicts the bounded-body movement and the red-team claim that the cap prevents memory consumption (`plan.md:84,92`; `subtasks/...g02-authenticate-and-parse.md:12-14`). Replace this with a single-consumption streaming reader that stops and cancels once the accumulated exact bytes exceed 256 KiB, preserving the accepted bytes unchanged for HMAC. Extend criterion 4's observable pass condition and route tests to prove `413`, zero candidate decryption/HMAC, zero parse/write/fetch, and early stream cancellation for an oversized chunked/no-`Content-Length` request. Keep the cheap declared-length precheck, but do not treat it as enforcement.

## Round 2 — 2026-07-17

### Plan summary

Round 1's blocking body-limit issue is resolved in the plan: the route now performs a cheap declared-length precheck and then consumes the body once with a streaming reader, preserving accepted chunks byte-for-byte and cancelling immediately when accumulated bytes cross 256 KiB before concatenation. Criterion 4 and groups g02/g03 require a chunked request without `Content-Length` to return empty `413`, prove early cancellation/unread tail, and perform zero candidate decrypt/HMAC, parse, write, or fetch. The HMAC, delivery ledger, one-GET reconciliation, timing, ACL, DOX, and spec scope is otherwise unchanged. Full plan: [[2.3.1-verify-and-handle-nautt-webhooks.plan]].

### Resposta do crítico (yolo)

Approved. The revised route and g02/g03 movements enforce the actual-byte ceiling before concatenation or authentication: declared oversize cancels an untouched body, streamed oversize counts before copying and cancels on the first crossing chunk, and only bounded accepted chunks become the byte-exact HMAC input. Criterion 4 observably requires chunked and dishonest/missing-`Content-Length` coverage, unread-tail proof, early cancellation, empty `413`, and zero decrypt/HMAC, parse, write, or fetch. The full gate also passes: every criterion has an executable run and observable pass condition; movements, forks, counterplays, and aborts are blind-executable; the approved Nautt spec/synthesis are linked; red-team and environment preflight are recorded; there are no `(user)` actions; scope is proportional to size L; and focused re-runs add security/concurrency evidence not supplied by the aggregate gate.
