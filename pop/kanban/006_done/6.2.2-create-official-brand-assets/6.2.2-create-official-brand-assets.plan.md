# Plan — [[6.2.2-create-official-brand-assets]]

- **Stage:** 002_planning · **Responsible:** planning agent

## Objective and observable result

- **Objective:** establish one official QR Pagamentos identity family for browser chrome, login, role-shell navigation, and the merchant-logo fallback without changing authentication, authorization, storefront data, or any other business capability.
- **Observable result:** the application ships an original canonical mark, reusable accessible lockups, deterministic favicon/raster derivatives, and a machine-checked asset manifest; every current and future consumer follows the same sizing, theme, fallback, and provenance rules.

## Strategy and fixed contract

Use one original, vector-first geometric mark derived from the existing `PIX settlement desk` language: QR alignment, receipt rail, and precise settlement rather than a literal scannable QR code. The canonical geometry is repository-owned SVG/source, with no copied trademark, stock asset, remote source, embedded executable content, or external reference. Final artwork is not generated by an image model: the image-generation workflow is useful for raster illustration and exploration, while this production logo needs deterministic code-native vector output and auditable provenance. Any exploratory output is excluded from the repository and cannot be traced or shipped.

The family has four stable usage identities: mark-only, horizontal product lockup, compact role-shell lockup, and merchant fallback. Lockups compose the canonical mark with the exact product name `QR Pagamentos` in the already licensed local IBM Plex Sans family; they do not bake translated text into images. The merchant fallback is the product mark shown only when no merchant logo is available and never impersonates merchant ownership. Task 6.2.3 consumes the compact lockup in its sidebars; this task supplies and verifies that contract but does not build either role shell.

Inline UI variants are monochrome and inherit one semantic foreground, so all six themes change tokens rather than asset geometry. A fixed positive/reversed export pair exists only where an external/static file cannot inherit UI tokens. The favicon retains a bounded high-contrast field at 16/32/48 pixels and has a vector source plus deterministic raster/ICO derivatives. No per-theme logo fork or component class branch is permitted.

A versioned manifest is the asset source of truth. It records stable ID, role, canonical source or composition, output path, MIME, intrinsic dimensions/viewBox, color mode, accessibility mode, provenance/license statement, and SHA-256 for every shipped file. A project command validates closed IDs, safe SVG structure, exact names/dimensions, hashes, favicon frames, and unreferenced or duplicate outputs; generated files carry an explicit generated marker and are never edited directly. Usage rules live in `DESIGN.md` and the brand subtree contract, not in page-local comments.

Accessibility follows context: a mark beside visible `QR Pagamentos` or merchant text is hidden from assistive technology; a standalone meaningful lockup has one localized accessible name; the SVG has no duplicate title announcement. The visible mark/wordmark reaches 3:1 non-text and 4.5:1 text contrast against every supported theme surface, remains recognizable in forced-colors/high-contrast presentation, and does not rely on color to distinguish variants. Minimum rendered sizes and clear space are manifest-backed and exercised at favicon, login, compact shell, and merchant-fallback scales.

## Risks and abort conditions

- **Identity/provenance risk:** an attractive external or generated concept cannot establish clean authorship. Mitigation: original documented geometry, repository-local inputs, dependency license inventory, and no generated/stock final art.
- **Small-size risk:** QR-like detail collapses or resembles a functional code. Mitigation: a non-scannable simplified mark, pixel-scale raster inspection, and closed favicon frame checks.
- **Theme risk:** fixed brand colors lose contrast in one of six themes. Mitigation: semantic monochrome inline variants, explicit static positive/reversed contexts, and automated contrast plus screenshot review.
- **Scope risk:** “sidebar” or “merchant logo” expands into future navigation or upload behavior. Mitigation: ship the reusable compact/fallback contract only; 6.2.3 owns shells and media/catalog tasks own uploads.
- **Abort if:** clean provenance cannot be demonstrated, the final mark is materially similar to a known inspected source, deterministic outputs cannot be reproduced/verified, or browser evidence cannot cover every theme and required size. Record the exact blocker; do not substitute an unlicensed or unverified asset.
