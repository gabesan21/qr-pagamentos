## Round 1 — 2026-07-24

### Resposta do crítico (yolo)

- **Context:** strong independent.
- **Return:** 1 of 2.
- **Decision:** returned → 002.
- **Original request:** the plan directly addresses the requested durable non-root media volume and the complete install, update, uninstall, backup, restore, recovery, and runbook lifecycle. Its pair-consistency, exact-release, fail-closed identity, no-sudo, no-public-database, redaction, retention, and disposable-resource goals are correctly scoped to the production read-only filesystem problem.
- **Resolved strengths:** the plan preserves the integrated 6.3.2 descriptor/digest and local-POSIX boundary; keeps the old app healthy through candidate build and additive migration; rejects partial restore modes; rehearses before managed mutation; preserves volume identities; retains a pre-restore recovery pair; makes purge explicit and paired; serializes the four write sets; and requires real Docker evidence rather than simulated PASS.
- **Concrete blockers:**
  1. The volume-access model contradicts the operations it must implement. The fixed topology says the media volume mounts read-write only into `app`, that no one-shot service receives it, and that only the non-root application is writable; candidate preflight, backup archive creation, restore rehearsal, managed restore, and recovery necessarily require ephemeral helpers to mount that same volume, with restore/recovery requiring write access. Define the closed exception explicitly: which operation-locked helper image/user may receive read-only versus read-write mounts, its network/secret/root-filesystem restrictions, how exact volume identity is rechecked immediately before access, and how helper containers are inventoried and removed. Keep the steady-state Compose topology app-only.
  2. The pre-promotion failure invariant is impossible after migration begins. The plan says *any* failure before app promotion leaves both volume contents, logs, and protected evidence unchanged, but the additive migration runs before promotion and deliberately changes PostgreSQL contents; failure logs and append-only operation evidence must also be retained. Split the contract by boundary: pre-migration failures leave the pair unchanged; post-migration/pre-promotion failures retain the additive database state without reversal while the exact old app remains healthy and media bytes remain unchanged; prior evidence is immutable while a redacted failure record/log is retained.
  3. Default uninstall/reinstall does not close credential continuity for the preserved PostgreSQL volume. The plan explicitly validates the same volumes and Nautt key, but the existing database roles also depend on the installed PostgreSQL administrator, migrator, and runtime password artifacts. A reinstall that rewrites any of those from edited environment input can strand the retained database even though volume/key checks pass. Require reuse and exact continuity validation of every protected installed credential artifact before any deployment mutation, forbid silent overwrite/regeneration when retained data exists, and add mismatch/refusal plus successful reinstall coverage without placing any credential or digest in manifests/logs.
  4. AC3 is not fully executable: `install/test.sh plus the focused installer fixture` leaves the second command/fixture unnamed. Make the fixture part of `install/test.sh` or provide its exact command, and require it to cover the credential-continuity refusal above together with paired purge, foreign-volume refusal, and zero-row-only legacy adoption.
